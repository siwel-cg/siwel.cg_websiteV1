Monte Carlo Path Tracer

Completed: January 2025
  • C++
  • GLSL

Overview

This was one of the major projects for CIS 5610. It is a fully featured Monte Carlo path tracer that simulates realistic lighting. Leveraging multiple importance sampling (MIS) and cosine-weighted sampling, the path tracer efficiently converges toward physically accurate global illumination. A JSON-based scene importer allows custom scenes—complete with lights, materials, and geometry—to be easily made and rendered.

Features

Multiple Importance Sampling (MIS)

To reduce noise and speed up convergence, each bounce in the scene leverages both BSDF sampling and direct light sampling. MIS balances these different strategies, ensuring that neither approach dominates, improving the quality of global illumination without a large increase in render time.

Global Illumination & Bounce Handling

For each ray bounce, the path tracer computes both the material’s BSDF contribution and direct lighting from the light sources. Cosine-weighted sampling helps accurately model diffuse reflections and reduce noise in indirect lighting. Rays continue bouncing until they hit a light source or exceed a bounce limit, capturing complex light interactions within the scene.

Material Models

The path tracer supports a variety of surface types:

  • Lambertian (Diffuse): Perfectly diffuse reflection.
  • Perfect Mirrors: Perfect reflection.
  • Microfacet: Glossy or rough reflections via micro-surface distribution.
  • Dielectric: Fresnel-based reflection/refraction for glass-like materials.

Light Types

The path tracer includes multiple ways to illuminate a scene:

  • Environment Light (HDR): An HDR map that wraps the entire scene.
  • Sphere & Area Lights: Extended emitters for softer shadows.
  • Point & Spot Lights: Localized emitters for sharper or focused lighting.

Scene & Asset Management

Custom scenes are specified via JSON, defining lights, geometry, materials, and textures. This modular approach lets you import different configurations or experimental setups without recompiling, ensuring flexibility and faster iteration on scene design.
